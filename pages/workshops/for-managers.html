Lead your team through it

You are the single biggest reason this sticks or stalls. People take their cue on a change like this from their direct manager, not from a portal. Here's the short version of your role — five actions, a script, and how to handle the hard question.

For managers · ~5 min read
Why you

Your team reads you

Change-management research is unambiguous: employees prefer to hear about a change like this from their immediate manager, and active manager support is one of the top predictors of adoption. A few small, visible signals from you outweigh any amount of program comms.

What to do

Five actions across the engagement

01
Before — protect the time
Hold the workshop slots on calendars and mean it. Tell your team the 10 minutes of pre-work is worth it. Point them to Why Cowork and the Rules of the Road.
02
Before — make it safe
Say out loud that this is about giving them hours back, not measuring or replacing them, and that experimenting (and stumbling) is expected.
03
During — bring real work
Encourage each person to bring an actual task from their week to each session. The value only clicks on their own files, not a demo.
04
During — ask about it
In your 1:1s, ask "what did you try in Cowork this week?" The question alone signals it matters and keeps it on the radar.
05
After — recognize and nominate
Call out a win or two by name. Nominate one or two people as champions to carry it forward once the formal sessions end.
If you want a script

What to say to your team

You don't need this — but if a 30-second version helps, here it is.

🗣️
30-second version

"We're doing four short workshops on Cowork over the next few weeks. The point is simple: give you back hours on the document-heavy work so your time goes to the judgment calls only you can make. Bring a real task to each session. There's one ground rule about what data we can and can't use — read it first. I'm bought in, and I'll be asking how it's going."

The question you'll get

"Is this coming for my job?"

Don't dodge it. The honest, accurate answer is also the reassuring one.

Say this

It augments, it doesn't replace

Cowork drafts; a person reviews, decides, and signs off. The judgment, the relationships, and the accountability stay with the team. We're investing in people with this skill, not measuring them by it.

The people who learn to direct it well become more valuable, not less.
Don't say this

Avoid the efficiency framing

Skip "do more with fewer people." It's the fastest way to kill desire and drive quiet non-adoption. Lead with time back and better work, not headcount.

If you're unsure how to answer something, point them to the FAQ.
