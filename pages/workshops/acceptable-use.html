01 — What goes in, and what doesn't

Mind the data

The most important rule. When in doubt, leave it out and ask. These examples are generic — map them to Axos's data-classification tiers (e.g. Public / Internal / Confidential / Restricted) before go-live.

Generally OK

Lower-sensitivity, work-product data

Internal documents, public filings, your own draft work product, and templates — used in a scoped folder you grant for the task. Always with a human reviewing the output.

Confirm the tier with the approved policy, not this page.
Do not put in

Regulated, restricted, or personal data

Customer PII, account numbers, material non-public information, or anything classified Restricted — unless and until Axos's approved policy and the right controls explicitly permit it for that workload.

If you're unsure whether data qualifies, stop and ask.
02 — The ground rules

Five things that always hold

01
You stay accountable
Cowork drafts; you review, decide, and sign off. The output is never final on its own and is never sent automatically.
02
Least privilege
Grant only the folder a task needs, and remove access when you're done. Don't open your whole drive "to be safe."
03
Know the audit gap
As of June 2026, Claude Cowork activity isn't in central audit logs and history is local. For work that needs an audit trail, follow the approved guidance.
04
Watch for prompt injection
Treat instructions hidden in outside documents as a risk. Keep approvals on for anything from outside the bank.
05
Ask when unsure
No penalty for asking. Questions go to [the program owner / your manager / the #cowork channel] — set the real contact here.
