Participant FAQ
01 — The honest questions

Let's start with the real ones

Is this coming for my job?
No. Cowork drafts; you review, decide, and sign off — the judgment, the relationships, and the accountability stay with you. Axos is investing in you with this skill, not measuring you by it. The people who learn to direct it well become more valuable, not less. See the straight answer on jobs.
Will I be tracked or measured on how much I use it?
This program isn't a surveillance exercise. We look at things like attendance and whether the workshops landed, in aggregate, to improve the program — not to score individuals. Your in-site progress and quizzes are stored on your own device.
Is this mandatory?
Your manager and sponsor will confirm the expectation for your team. Either way, the ask is small and concrete: attend four 2-hour sessions, do ten minutes of pre-work each, and try it on real work.
I'm not technical — is this really for me?
Yes — it's built for knowledge workers, not coders. There's no terminal and no code. You describe a result in plain language and review what comes back. If you can write an email and open a folder, you can do this.
02 — Your data & what's allowed

Safety and the ground rules

Is my data safe?
Claude Cowork runs locally on your machine and only sees the folders you grant it, and on Axos's commercial plan your data isn't used to train models. That said, the binding answer for what you may use it on is the Rules of the Road, once Legal, Compliance, and InfoSec have signed it off.
What data can I put into Cowork?
Follow the Rules of the Road. The short version: lower-sensitivity, internal work product is generally fine in a scoped folder; regulated, restricted, or personal data (customer PII, account numbers, MNPI) stays out unless the approved policy explicitly permits it. When in doubt, leave it out and ask.
Can I use it on customer or client information?
Not until Axos's approved policy says so for that specific workload. This is exactly the kind of thing the Rules of the Road and your Compliance team define — don't guess.
What's this "audit gap" I keep hearing about?
As of June 2026, Claude Cowork activity isn't captured in central audit logs and history lives locally on your machine. It's a known limitation we manage with scope, approvals, and monitoring — not a reason to avoid it for appropriate work. Workshop 4 and the Rules of the Road cover how.
03 — Getting it working

The practical stuff

Do I have access? Do I need to install anything?
Confirm your Claude Cowork access as part of the pre-work for Workshop 1. If it's not provisioned, flag it to the program team before the session so we can sort it out — don't wait until you're in the room.
I sign in with SSO — does everything work?
Almost everything. One advanced step in Workshop 3 (the skill-creator's automatic description optimizer) needs a raw API key, which SSO-only sign-in doesn't provide. You can tune by hand instead — the facilitator will show you. Nothing else is affected.
Which model should I use, and does it cost the bank money?
Default to Sonnet — it handles most work at much lower cost, and Cowork uses more of your allowance than chat does. Reserve Opus for genuinely hard reasoning. Workshop 2 covers this; cost discipline is a habit we teach from day one.
What if I miss a workshop?
Everything for each session lives on its workshop hub — the lessons, the lab, and the slides — so you can catch up, and bring questions to office hours. Tell the program team so we can help you stay on track.
04 — During & after

Help, and what counts

Where do I get help after the sessions?
Office hours, your champions, and the #cowork channel — set the real contacts here. The resource library has the skills, templates, and decks in one place.
Is the certificate official?
Not yet. The certificate on My Progress is a personal record on your device to mark that you finished — it's a motivator, not an HR or compliance record. If completion needs to count formally, that runs through Axos's official system (e.g. the LMS), which the program team is sorting out separately.
What if Cowork gets something wrong?
Expect it sometimes — that's why every output is a draft you review before anything is used or sent. You stay in the loop and you stay accountable. Catching and correcting is part of the workflow, not a failure of it.
