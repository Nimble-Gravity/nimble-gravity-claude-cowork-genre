Governance for IT

This is the page for the person who has to sign off — the IT and compliance owner. How Cowork is enabled and controlled, where its audit coverage stands today, and how the data is handled. Get this right and the rollout has air cover; get it wrong and it stalls in review.

Plugins & Rollout · ~20 min
01 — Enable and manage it

Turn it on, and keep it under control

Cowork is an admin-enabled capability, not a free-for-all. IT decides who gets it, ties it to identity, and pushes policy to managed devices.

01
Enable org-wide
Turn Cowork on under Organization settings, then Capabilities. It's off until you enable it, so nothing is exposed before you decide to.
02
Tie it to identity
Manage access with SSO and SCIM, so accounts follow your directory and deprovisioning a user removes their access too.
03
Push desktop policy
Use MDM — Jamf, Kandji, or Intune — to deploy and configure the desktop app on managed machines instead of relying on each user.
02 — The audit-coverage gap, and how to manage it

The one fact every regulated rollout must address

This is the deciding point for a regulated bank, so be precise about it. As of June 2026, Claude Cowork's activity isn't centrally auditable — so you manage it deliberately, with scope, policy, and monitoring.

The gap

Activity isn't in the audit logs yet

As of June 2026, Claude Cowork activity is not in Anthropic's Compliance API or audit logs, and history lives locally on the user's machine — it can't be centrally managed or exported. Anthropic is actively working to close this; re-verify it each cycle.

Treat it as a known limitation to manage, not a blocker.
How you manage it

Scope, policy, and monitoring

Govern it with the controls you do have: least-privilege folder access, approvals on for sensitive work, and usage monitoring through the admin dashboard and Analytics API. For work that needs zero-retention or centralized audit today, use Anthropic's audited surfaces — the API or Claude Code Enterprise.

Scope tightly; keep approvals on; watch the analytics.
03 — Data and retention

What's kept, for how long, and what isn't

The questions a CISO asks first. The commercial answer is reassuring, with one limit worth stating plainly so it doesn't surface later as a surprise.

No training, 30-day retention

On commercial plans — Team, Enterprise, and API — Anthropic doesn't train on your data by default, and retention is 30 days.

ZDR has a boundary

Zero Data Retention is available for the API and Claude Code Enterprise, but not for the Team or Enterprise Cowork interface. State that limit up front for regulated workloads.

🛡️
What to put in front of your CISO

One page: Cowork is admin-enabled, identity-bound through SSO/SCIM, and MDM-managed; commercial data isn't trained on and is kept 30 days; access is least-privilege with approvals on for sensitive work; and the audit-coverage gap is tracked and re-verified each cycle. That's the governance case the capstone asks you to make.
