Get Set Up

Cowork works out of the box, but its answers stay generic until it knows your role, your writing, and the tools you use. A guided setup of 20 to 40 minutes fixes that. You don't fill in forms. A short interview asks you questions and writes the configuration for you.

Setup & Foundations · ~20 min
01 — What the setup produces

A few files that travel with you

The setup writes a small set of configuration files that shape every later session. The cowork-cosetup interview generates them from your answers, so you don't have to write any of it yourself.

cowork-instructions.md

The file Cowork reads at the start of every session. It holds your standing context, which is what gets Cowork writing in your register from the first reply.

An about-me/ folder

Five files cover your role and tools (about-me), how you write (voice-profile), what to avoid (writing-rules), and your people and active work (team and memory, which Cowork keeps current).

⏱️
Before you start

You'll need access to Cowork and 20 to 40 minutes. You can stop and pick up where you left off. Connect your M365 first so the interview can pull from your real email, Teams, and calendar instead of starting cold.

02 — Connect your data sources

Connectors are part of setup, not an afterthought

Cowork is far more useful when it can see your real work. Connect your data sources before the interview so it can pre-fill from your email, calendar, and files instead of starting cold.

Microsoft 365 shops

Turn on the M365 connector. An admin grants consent once; it then connects with delegated permissions, so Cowork sees only what you can see, and Graph calls are logged in your M365 audit log. In Cowork, open Settings, then Connectors, add Microsoft 365, and approve the scopes.

Google and other shops

Not on Microsoft? Connect Google Drive, Gmail, and Calendar the same way, from Settings, then Connectors. Local-files-only is also fine: grant a folder and skip connectors for now, then add them as new tasks call for it.

🔌
Least privilege from the start

Connectors reach your services through Anthropic's cloud with delegated permissions scoped to you. Connect what the work needs and no more, and add connectors later as new tasks call for them.

03 — Installing the co-setup skill

Drop it in your skills folder and run it

The cowork-cosetup folder holds a SKILL.md and a short guide. Get the folder, put it in your Claude Cowork skills folder, then run it from a fresh session.

Step 1

Find your skills folder

Ask Cowork to "list my skills" to find the folder, or "set up my skills folder" if you don't have one yet. Drop cowork-cosetup in.

Installing your first skill is what creates the skills folder.
Step 2

Run it from a fresh session

Open a new session and run /cowork-cosetup. If the interview can't pull anything in, check that your connectors are on so it can read your real work.

Cowork picks up new skills only when a session starts.
🧭
If you get stuck

Say "list my skills" in any session and Cowork tells you what's installed and where the folder is. Cowork only picks up new skills when a session starts, so always run one from a fresh chat after you add it.

04 — The co-setup skill

Copy the skill, paste it into Cowork

The quickest way to install cowork-cosetup is to copy the skill below, paste it into a fresh Cowork session, and ask Claude to "make this a skill." Downloading the file is the secondary path, for re-runs or for setting someone else up.

cowork-cosetup / SKILL.md 
---
name: cowork-cosetup
description: Interviews a new Claude Cowork user and writes their personalization files — cowork-instructions.md plus an about-me/ folder — so Cowork knows their role, voice, priorities, and team from the first session. Use at first-time setup, when onboarding someone to Claude Cowork, when a user asks to "set me up", "personalize Cowork", or run "co-setup", or whenever Cowork is giving generic, role-blind answers that ignore who the user is.
---

# Cowork Co-Setup

Run a short guided interview, then write the personalization files that make Cowork
play back who the user is and tailor its help to their actual job.

## What you produce

cowork-instructions.md       (read at the start of every session)
about-me/about-me.md         (role, responsibilities, priorities, tools)
about-me/voice-profile.md    (how the user writes and wants drafts to sound)
about-me/writing-rules.md    (do / don't rules for any drafted output)
about-me/team.md             (living: who they work with and how)
about-me/memory.md           (living: durable facts worth remembering)

These are context files, not skills — they live alongside your Claude Cowork skills
folder. Installing your first skill is what creates that folder.

## The interview

1. Pre-fill, don't interrogate. If an M365 connector is available, read name, role,
   and team first and confirm them rather than asking cold.
2. Adapt to the role — Delivery, Operations, Sales, Enablement, or Leadership.
3. Keep it to 20–40 minutes. Mark skips with [FILL IN]; never invent an answer.
4. Read it back before writing, so the user can correct it.

## After setup

Start a fresh session and run two prompts: "Recap what you know about me." and
"Given who I am, what can you help me with?" If the playback is generic, open the
relevant file, fix it, and re-run.

05 — How the interview works

It reads first, then asks

The interview is built to go quickly. It looks at your recent work before it asks anything, so most questions arrive with an answer already filled in for you to confirm or change.

01
It pre-fills from your work
Before any questions, it scans your role, your most frequent collaborators, and how you write, then shows you what it found to confirm or correct.
02
It follows your role
Early on it asks whether your work is delivery, operations, sales, enablement, leadership, or a mix, and the rest of the questions follow from your answer.
03
You can skip anything
Pick "Skip, fill in later" and it leaves a [FILL IN] marker you can find afterward. A setup with a few gaps still beats one you never finish.
✅
When it finishes

The files are live straight away, with nothing to switch on. Read back voice-profile.md and writing-rules.md and adjust anything that doesn't sound like you; those two are the ones worth getting right. team.md and memory.md stay current on their own as you work.

06 — Verify it worked

A 60-second check

Before you move on, confirm the setup actually took. Three quick checks.

01
The skill ran
Ask Cowork to "list my skills" and confirm cowork-cosetup is installed and has run.
02
The files are there
Check that cowork-instructions.md and the about-me/ folder were written, and skim voice-profile.md and writing-rules.md — those two are worth getting right.
03
Ready for your first session
If anything's missing, re-run /cowork-cosetup or edit the file directly. Then you're set for Lesson 3, where you put it to work.
