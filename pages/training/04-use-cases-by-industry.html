Use Cases by Industry

The quickest way to see what Cowork is worth is to point it at a job that matters in your own work. This lesson covers the tasks worth trying first in each industry, and the open-sourced packs from Anthropic you can build on instead of starting from nothing.

Use Cowork · ~20 min
01 — Start where it pays off

Pick a job that ends in a deliverable

In every industry, the best first tasks are the multi-step, multi-file jobs you already do by hand. They produce a finished artifact, and you can tell at a glance whether it's right.

Research and synthesis

Hand Cowork a set of sources and ask for a structured brief with citations. It pulls the relevant points out of a stack of documents and gives you back something you can review.

Document and data prep

Pull source files into a draft, build a working Excel model, or turn a pile of contracts and reports into something clean you can actually read.

02 — By industry

Build on Anthropic's open-sourced packs

Anthropic has open-sourced first-party skill and plugin packs for regulated work, all built so that the agent drafts and a person signs off. Seed your industry labs from these rather than from a blank page.

Financial services

Banking, equity research, PE, wealth

The anthropics/financial-services pack includes reference agents like a Pitch Agent, a GL Reconciler, and a KYC Screener. Every output is staged for a person to approve, and nothing trades or posts on its own.

Available as a Cowork plugin or through the Managed Agents API.
Legal

Contract and NDA work

The anthropics/claude-for-legal marketplace ships plugins such as /review-contract and /triage-nda. A cold-start interview writes a practice profile the other skills read, and the result is always a draft for an attorney to review.

The same draft-and-review pattern fits insurance and healthcare.
03 — See it on your own work

Run a starter against your files

A demo on sample data proves nothing about your job. Run a starter task on your own files and watch how the output reflects your context. That's the moment a curious user turns into a daily one.
