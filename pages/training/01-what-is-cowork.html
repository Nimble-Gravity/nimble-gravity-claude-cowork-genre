What Is Cowork?

Cowork puts the agent that powers Claude Code into the Claude desktop app, with no terminal to learn. You hand it a multi-step job and it does the work while you supervise. This program is built entirely on Claude Cowork from Anthropic, and grounded in Anthropic's own guidance for using it well.

Setup & Foundations · ~20 min
01 — Chat vs. Cowork

How Cowork is different from a chat

In a chat you go back and forth on a question and then copy the answer somewhere useful. In Cowork you describe the result you want and Claude works across your files to produce it, checking in as it goes. The output is usually a finished document or spreadsheet rather than a transcript you still have to act on.

In a chat

You and Claude talk through a problem. It can't open your files, so anything it suggests, you carry out yourself. That's a good fit for thinking out loud, drafting, and quick answers.

In a Cowork session

You give Claude a goal and access to a set of files. It reads them, edits and creates what it needs, and hands the work back for you to review.

💡
Why people miss this at first

Anthropic's own guidance puts it bluntly: without training, people use Cowork the way they use chat and never see the difference. Helping your team make that shift is where most of the value comes from, and it's what the rest of this program is about.

02 — Where it runs

Local, with guardrails

Claude Cowork is the Claude Code agent inside the Claude desktop app on macOS and Windows, made for people who don't write code. It plans the work, splits it into steps, and can run several streams at once. It does all of that on your own machine.

Your files

It only touches folders you grant

Claude reads, edits, and creates files inside the folders you connect, and nothing outside them. It also checks with you before it deletes anything permanently. You control its reach by choosing the folder.

The app has to stay open and the machine awake while a task runs.
Code in a sandbox

It runs code in an isolated VM

When a task needs code or a shell command, Cowork runs it in a separate virtual machine on your computer. That's how it can write working Excel formulas, decks, and documents without having free rein over your system.

04 — Where it fits your day

Good first tasks at Gen Re

The fastest way to feel the difference is to hand off something real and document-heavy. These are the kinds of tasks that pay off first in reinsurance — each one a draft an underwriter, actuary, or claims professional reviews and signs off.

Summarize

Turn a cedent submission pack, a claims file, or a stack of experience data into a one-page summary with a cited findings list.

Draft

Take notes or an email thread and draft a memo in your format and voice. The structure rarely changes, so it's an ideal early win.

Compare & organize

Line up treaty terms across a portfolio of contracts, or triage a claims queue into a prioritized brief. Repetitive, document-heavy work is where it shines.
