The Control Room

Internal Audit gave notice: a Cowork control review starts in 40 minutes. The pilot ran hot — folders scoped to whole drives, roles copied from a template, spend spiking on one desk, and nobody has written the control narrative. Four open exceptions stand between your team and an exam-ready evidence pack.

Capstone exercise · ~40 min
01 — The scenario

Forty minutes to exam-ready

The room is a game; the evidence pack is not. Each station hands you the work a real control review would demand — an access review, a role-matrix repair, an adoption readout, and the one-page control narrative — and the output of that work contains the sign-off code that clears the next station. The capstone deliverable is produced inside the game, under a clock.

Form a team of 2–4

One driver in Cowork, everyone on the problem. Mixed teams work best — put an IT or compliance partner with the business users. Enter a team name and the clock starts.

Control Room + Cowork side by side

Keep the game in one window and Cowork in the other. Each station's panel carries the review procedure and the code format; download the file pack before the floor opens.

Hints cost time

Two hints per station: the first is free, the second adds 2 minutes to your final time. Wrong codes cost nothing but pride — attempts are counted.

📁
Lab file pack — download before you open the Control Room

Six files: a synthetic Axos access register, the two written standards you review against, last week's analytics export, and Internal Audit's acceptance checklist. Nothing here is real Axos data — put them in one folder and grant Cowork access to that folder only.

02 — The four stations

Four stations, four governance disciplines

Each station exercises one thing Lessons 1–4 taught, in order of difficulty. By the last station you have carried out a full control review: scope it, fix the roles, measure it, then write the page that defends it.

01
The Access Map
Eighteen folder grants, one written rubric, and no review on file. Delegate the access review and count what fails. Proves: folder scope is the data control — there is no finer-grained permission to hide behind.
02
The Broken Role Matrix
A role design drafted in a hurry: everyone an Owner, a custom role that grants nothing, a nested IdP group that will never sync, a connector on Always allow. Repair it against the standard. Proves: RBAC is a design, not a checkbox.
03
Read the Room
Author a cowork-adoption-review skill, then run it on last week's analytics export to find the weekly actives and the desk burning the budget. Proves: adoption is measured, and model spend is capped — not noted.
04
The Control Narrative
Chain all three artifacts into the one-page control narrative, then score it against Internal Audit's nine-item acceptance checklist until it clears. Proves: evidence is not an answer — the page is the deliverable.
03 — What the Control Room is really testing

The game is a wrapper for governance discipline

Every code is gated on a control a regulated rollout actually has to demonstrate — and the Room 4 narrative is a document you could put in front of a sponsor on Monday.

Stations 1–2 · Control design

Scope and roles are the whole game

Station 1 makes you test real grants against a written standard; Station 2 makes you repair a role matrix carrying every common misconfiguration — blanket Owner, a Custom role that inherits nothing, an unsyncable nested group, an org-wide connector left on Always allow.

Least privilege is a folder choice. Delegation is a Custom role, not an Owner seat.
Stations 3–4 · Proving it

Measure it, then defend it

Station 3 only opens if your skill aggregates the export correctly; Station 4 only opens on a clean sweep of the acceptance checklist — including naming where Cowork's audit coverage ends and what compensates for it.

A finding that says "monitor Opus spend" without a model cap is not remediated.
04 — After the review (homework)

Take the narrative to your sponsor

The Control Room used a synthetic Axos pilot; your homework uses the real one. The one-pager you produced in Station 4 is the template — re-run it against your own environment.

01
Re-run the access review
Export your own Cowork folder grants and run the Station 1 review against the rubric. Bring the count of non-compliant grants — that number is your opening argument for a scoped pilot.
02
Draft the real role matrix
Map your pilot group to Custom roles and the six admin permission areas. Remember that Custom roles grant nothing implicitly, and confirm your IdP groups are direct-member, not nested.
03
Ship the one-pager
Score your real control narrative against exam-checklist.txt until all nine items pass, then take it to your sponsor and your IT partner. Bring it to office hours if you want a second read.

Re-verify before you circulate it

The audit-coverage position in this workshop was verified against Anthropic's documentation on 21 July 2026, and Anthropic is actively working to close it. Before your control narrative goes to a committee, re-check the current state against the Sources in cowork-context.md and re-date the page. A governance document with a stale date is the one finding you cannot argue with.
