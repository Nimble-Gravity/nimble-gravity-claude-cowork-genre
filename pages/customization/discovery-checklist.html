Discovery Checklist

A short session, run with the client the week before the workshop, that decides how the program is tailored. Its answers fill the client-swappable slots: the industry use cases, the lab scenario, the top-5 list, and which skills to skin. This is the generic template — capture the answers, then customize on a client branch.

1–2 hours · facilitator + client sponsor
01 — Audience & maturity

Who is this for, and where are they starting?

The audience and its maturity set the level of every lesson and lab. Get specific — "the secondaries analysts" beats "the investment team."

01
Who is rolling out — team or org?
A single team's pilot and an org-wide rollout are different engagements. Name the first cohort and who sponsors it.
02
What do those people do all day?
The real, repetitive, document-heavy tasks. This is the raw material for the use cases and the labs.
03
What's their AI maturity?
Curious, exploring, piloting, scaling, or governed? This sets the pace and seeds the opening maturity poll.
02 — Stack & licenses

Which surfaces, and what's licensed?

The stack decides the setup track and the connector walkthroughs; the licenses decide which products you can actually demo on day one.

Stack

Microsoft, Google, or mixed?

A Microsoft shop gets the M365 connector walkthrough; a Google or mixed shop gets the Google connectors. Local-files-only is always a safe default if connectors aren't approved in time.

Plan a local-files-first lab path in case connectors lag.
Licenses

Which Claude products?

Claude Cowork, Claude Code, and the API — confirm what's actually licensed and provisioned, and whether SSO-only sign-in will block the skill-creator optimizer.

Confirm; don't assume. The demo depends on it.
03 — Use cases & champions

The first wins, and who will carry them

Three to five candidate use cases give the labs something real to chew on, and two or three champions per team carry the momentum after you leave.

01
3–5 candidate use cases
Repetitive, document-heavy, ending in a deliverable. These seed the top-5 list and the lab scenario.
02
Likely champions
Who's already curious and respected on the team? They become the office-hours regulars and the internal owners.
03
Regulated boundaries
Which work is compliance-bound? That shapes how tightly you scope access and which workloads wait given the audit-coverage gap — confirm it early so the governance story is right.
