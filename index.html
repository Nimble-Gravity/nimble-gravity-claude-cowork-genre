What You'll Be Able to Do

From a first task to a governed rollout

Cowork takes Claude Code's agent and makes it usable by anyone: you describe a result and it works across your files to produce it. The program takes a team from "what is this" to using Cowork well, building reusable skills, and rolling it out with the governance and adoption tracking enterprises ask for. It's built entirely on Claude Cowork, with the enterprise controls a regulated bank needs.

Who it's for

Knowledge workers across Axos — commercial and consumer banking, lending, and treasury management, plus the risk, compliance, and operations teams behind them. No coding needed, and no prior agentic-AI experience assumed.

What you leave with

A personalized Cowork setup, working fluency on real industry tasks, one reusable skill built the way Anthropic recommends, and a governed rollout plan with an adoption dashboard design.

How the program runs

Four workshops, two hours each

Each module is delivered as one 2-hour, hands-on workshop. A little pre-work before, a small task after, and office hours alongside. Everything you need for a session lives on its workshop hub.

01
Before each session
Ten minutes of pre-work so you arrive ready — access confirmed and a real task in hand.
02
Two hours, hands-on
Teach, a live banking demo, discussion, then a 40-minute lab that ends in a real deliverable.
03
After, and alongside
A short homework task and office hours keep it moving between sessions.

Capstone

Roll It Out, Governed

The program ends with a deliverable a sponsor can act on, built in three moves.

01
Govern by workload
Decide which workloads run in Claude Cowork now and how each is scoped: least-privilege folders, the right permission mode, and which sensitive data waits given the June 2026 audit-coverage gap.
02
Design the dashboard
Pull the Enterprise Analytics API and OpenTelemetry into Power BI or Databricks for weekly active usage, per-user spend by model, and your AI champions.
03
Plan the rollout
Pilot, then department, then org, with two or three champions per team and All-Staff 101s where everyone delegates a real task, plus one reusable skill shipped.
