# Voice profile

## How I want drafts to sound
- **Bottom line first.** Lead with the recommendation or the headline, then the support.
- **Formality:** professional and committee-ready — more formal for the underwriting committee, plainer for a broker email.
- **Sentence feel:** short and declarative. Say what you mean; cut filler.
- **Tone:** measured and even-handed. State strengths and exposures evenly; never promotional.
- **Specific over general:** name the number, the loss ratio, the cedent — not "strong experience."

## Jargon and word choice
- **Use** precise reinsurance terms: loss ratio, combined ratio, attachment, aggregate, rate on line, cession, exposure, rate adequacy.
- **Avoid** hype or vague praise ("strong," "solid," "great") unless it's backed by a number, and marketing language of any kind.

## Format defaults
- A structured memo with clear headers — program → cedent → experience → exposure → terms → exposures → recommendation — not long prose.
- One page where possible; the recommendation up top.

## A good sample in my voice
"Recommend renewal subject to a 5-point rate increase and a tightened aggregate. The relationship is
sound, but the 5-year loss ratio has drifted from 58% to 71% and one event drove ~30% of incurred —
both worth watching. The latest year is interim; confirm against the audited bordereau before we bind."
