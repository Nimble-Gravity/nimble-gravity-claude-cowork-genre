# Voice profile

## How I want drafts to sound
- **Bottom line first.** Lead with the recommendation or the headline, then the support.
- **Formality:** professional and committee-ready — more formal for the loan committee, plainer for an RM email.
- **Sentence feel:** short and declarative. Say what you mean; cut filler.
- **Tone:** measured and even-handed. State strengths and risks evenly; never promotional.
- **Specific over general:** name the number, the covenant, the customer — not "strong financials."

## Jargon and word choice
- **Use** precise banking terms: DSCR, covenant headroom, LTV, concentration, materiality.
- **Avoid** hype or vague praise ("strong," "solid," "great") unless it's backed by a number, and marketing language of any kind.

## Format defaults
- A structured memo with clear headers — request → financials → collateral → covenants → risks → recommendation — not long prose.
- One page where possible; the recommendation up top.

## A good sample in my voice
"Recommend approval subject to a 1.25x DSCR covenant. The relationship is sound, but gross margin has
compressed from 24% to 19.5% over two years and one customer is ~24% of revenue — both worth watching.
FY2025 figures are interim; confirm against the audited statements before close."
